title: Wanting to Pivot
---

I'm only in my early 40's and I'm extremely exhausted with everything...

Not sure if this is what they call a "midlife crisis", but my life and work just feel devoid of meaning. While I don't hate what I do and I largely work with good people, the actual work doesn't have any benefit to society. Just corpos making corpo software for other corpos, now using tools made by the worst people imaginable.

I know that most people aren't even in a position to gripe about who benefits from their work. I feel it's from a place of immense privilege that I'm even able to think about this: I just wish there was a place to apply my skills that could actually help improve society somewhat, instead of perpetuating this self-aggrandizing technocracy.

Real meaningful work isn't done in software-land. You can't fix social problems or make life better with software alone, and most software needed for society to function and thrive has already been built. This is why technocrats are trying to invent new "paradigms" like Web3 and gen-AI to create new markets of demand for things we do not need.

What that means for me and specialists like me is that my niche skills in enterprise tech aren't really applicable outside of it in a meaningful way. Charities, non-profits, and public service don't have much need for a DevSecOps engineer whose primary skills involve maintaining build systems and shared services for a massive software company.

Honestly at this point I'm just kind of holding on until I can retire and maybe spend time working on open source projects, or reskill back into electronics and work on designing and repairing useful things.
